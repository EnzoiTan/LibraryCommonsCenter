Brief
History
of the
Library

Approximately nine decades ago, the Zamboanga City Polytechnic College had found its beginning in a small shop-work classroom of the Zamboanga Central School. That was in July, 1905. With the rise of its enrolment, it was converted into a provincial trade school, known as the Zamboanga Trade School, in 1921, becoming independent of its own in this particular year as it separated itself from its mother institution, the Zamboanga Central School. It is safe to say that alongside the founding of the institution is the establishment of the library, for without it the purpose of crafting the knowledge would be inadequate. The library is the crowning jewel of the institution that supports its academic formation and research development.

Through the efforts of the first and only Zamboangueno Senator, Roseller T. Lim, who sponsored a bill, the Republic Act. No. 846 was enacted into law in 1954 by the late Nationalist Leader of the Philippines, President Ramon F. Magsaysay. This law mandated the conversion of the city trade school into the Zamboanga City Regional School of Arts and Trades. This degree authorized the school to offer post-secondary courses in trade technical education. Mr. Marcelo Bonilla was its first superintendent. Narratives suggest that it was during this period that the library was formally established.

In 1992, the school enjoyed a milestone. Through a bill sponsored by Congresswoman Maria Clara L. Lobregat, Republic Act No. 7474 was effected. This law officially converted the name Zamboanga School of Arts and Trades to Zamboanga City Polytechnic College. The Library has expanded its collection and services on maritime education, industrial arts, and home economics that cater to the requirements needed for tertiary level.

In [1954] the library was situated on the 2nd floor of the old CAHSS Building (now the CICS Building). Mrs. Amerita, C. Abelon was the first librarian. Mrs. Henedina E. Fidel succeeded upon Mrs. Abelon's retirement.

In 2013, after the retirement of Mrs. Henedina E. Fidel, Mrs. Lourdes R. Alvarez was appointed as Head Librarian. During this period, the library relocated to CTE building and ballooned to 15,000 volumes of collection. After the retirement of Mrs. Alvarez in 2017, Dr. Nelson P. Cabral, the College President, appointed Mr. Carl Joseph C. Burlat as the new head librarian. In July 2019, the library relocated to its new location behind the CTE building. The library was later renamed into Learning Resource Center before it eventually settled to its present name, the Learning Commons Center under the supervision of Ms. Jenelee C. Pastor.

Today, the Learning Commons Center boosts a collection of almost 20,000 volumes of books.
